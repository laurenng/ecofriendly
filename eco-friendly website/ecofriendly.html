Facts

Since the Industrial Revolution, worldwide use of fossil fuels has skyrocketed.

The biggest person behind the rise of climate change was Thomas Midgeley Jr. (1889-1944), who developed leaded gasoline (made by adding tetraethyllead (TEL), to gasoline) The gasoline proved to be popular with car companies, who started using it widely. Eventually the negative health effects of the gasoline were discovered, and leaded gasoline is banned in most parts of the world.

Midgeley also worked on the first chloroflurocarbon (CFC), dichlorodifluromethane ("Freon") for use as a refrigerant. CFCs are now linked to ozone depletion.
